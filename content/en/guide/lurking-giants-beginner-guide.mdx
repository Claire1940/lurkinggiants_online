<Section icon={Zap} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics">

Understanding the fundamental systems is what separates survivors from victims. In this world, the giants operate on complex AI routines including sight, sound, and smell. Your UI provides critical feedback on your visibility and noise levels. Mastering these indicators is the heart of any **lurking giants beginner guide**.

| Mechanic | Description | Impact on Survival |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stealth Meter** | Indicates how visible you are | High visibility triggers Giant aggro |
| **Noise Indicator** | Shows sound generated by movement | Running attracts nearby entities |
| **Stamina Bar** | Governs sprinting and climbing | Empty bar leads to exhaustion |
| **Exposure** | Environmental temperature effects | Causes health decay over time |

Movement in this game is deliberate. You must learn to differentiate between the heavy footsteps of a "Colossus" and the lighter, more erratic movements of a "Scout." Scouts are the primary threat for beginners as they are faster and more likely to investigate small noises.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Avoid "Stamina Burn." If you use your entire bar, your character will enter a heavy breathing state, significantly increasing the noise you generate for 5 seconds.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="First Day Survival Walkthrough">

Your first 20 minutes are the most critical. You start with nothing and must quickly secure basic tools before the first giant patrol passes your spawn area. Follow these steps to ensure a successful start.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Scavenge Surface Materials">
    Look for loose branches and small stones on the ground. Do not attempt to chop trees until you have crafted your first stone axe, as punching trees causes minor health damage and excessive noise.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Craft Basic Tools">
    Open your crafting menu and prioritize the Stone Axe and the Crude Pickaxe. These allow you to gather resources 3x faster and with 50% less noise than using your hands.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Find High Ground">
    Locate a cliff or a tall ruin. Giants have poor vertical vision. Establishing a small lookout post early on helps you track their patrol paths without being seen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure Food and Water">
    Find a fresh water source. Berries are a safe early-game food, but they provide low satiety. Aim to trap small game once you have the materials for a basic snare.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Patrol Timing">
Giants usually follow a set pathing routine. Observe a giant for one full cycle (usually 3-5 minutes) to understand when it is safe to cross open fields.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Giant Encounter Strategies">

Inevitably, you will be spotted. Knowing how to react can mean the difference between a narrow escape and a total inventory loss. Giants are not invincible, but in the early game, they should be treated as environmental hazards rather than enemies to be fought.

**Giant Behavior Types:**
- **The Wanderer**: Moves aimlessly. High peripheral vision but low hearing.
- **The Sentinel**: Stays in one spot guarding high-value loot. Very high hearing.
- **The Tracker**: Specifically looks for player-made structures. Will follow scent trails.

| Action | Result | Recommended When |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Freeze** | Reduces visibility by 40% | Giant is looking in your direction |
| **Distraction** | Throws a rock to create noise | You need to clear a path |
| **Dive** | Quick move to prone | Giant is about to swing or stomp |
| **Smoke Bomb** | Breaks line of sight | You are cornered or trapped |

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Penalty">
Upon death, you drop all "Backpack Items." Your "Belt Items" (tools and weapons) remain with you. Always keep your most valuable tools in your belt slots!
</Callout>

</Section>
